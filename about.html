About SunRi-Ssakhi

A place to listen,
a place to be heard.

SunRi-Ssakhi is a community built for women who have always had something to say — and never quite had the space to say it freely.

SunRi-Ssakhi

In Hindi, Sakhi means friend — a companion, someone who walks alongside you. SunRi means listen. Together, SunRi-Ssakhi is an invitation: listen, friend. Because every woman's story deserves to be heard.

Why we exist

She was talented. She was beautiful.
She just did not know it yet.

The more women I met, the more I noticed something. We were going through similar things — the same quiet struggles, the same unspoken feelings — but we were not talking about them. Not really. Every woman I encountered had something remarkable in her, but she could not see it herself.

SunRi-Ssakhi exists because expression is how we find ourselves. The more we share, the more clearly we see who we are.

Write freely

Share your thoughts, your dreams, what you are going through — without fear of judgement. In any language. At any stage of life.

Find yourself

Expression is not just sharing — it is discovery. When you write, you often find clarity you did not know you were looking for.

Be seen

For women in careers, at home, returning after a break, raising families, rebuilding themselves — every story matters here.

No judgement

This is a space without comparison, without criticism. Just women holding space for each other, honestly.

AJ

Founder

Ammbica Jain

IT Professional  ·  Transformational Coach  ·  Lifelong learner

I spent my whole career in IT. Then I took a break — nearly eight to nine years — to take care of my family. During that time, and after moving to the United States, I kept feeling like something was missing. Not in my life, exactly. In my voice.

I had things to say. I just did not have a place to say them. And when I started meeting other women, I realised I was not alone in that feeling. We were all carrying something — quietly, beautifully, without anywhere to put it down.

I am also a Transformational Coach, and the mind has always fascinated me — how we think, how we hold ourselves back, how expression can unlock something in us that nothing else can. SunRi-Ssakhi came out of that belief: that when a woman starts to speak, she starts to find herself.

This community is for every woman — in a job, at home, between chapters, from anywhere in the world. You do not need a perfect story. You just need to begin.

"The more we share, the more clearly we see who we are."

— Ammbica Jain, Founder of SunRi-Ssakhi
